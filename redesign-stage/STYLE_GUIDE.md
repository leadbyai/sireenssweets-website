Non-production redesign research/staging notes. Live main site was not changed.

## Patterns worth borrowing

- **Decision-first header:** users should instantly choose Call, Start a Custom Order, Pricing, Gallery, or Allergy-Friendly Options.
- **Bigger food photography:** premium bakeries lead with close, high-quality product photography and avoid making the logo/nav the whole experience.
- **Occasion-based navigation:** birthdays, weddings, baby showers, logo/corporate cookies, allergy-friendly desserts — this matches how customers shop.
- **Product/category cards above the fold:** cards make the site feel more shoppable and reduce mobile wandering.
- **Craft + human story:** chef/local-owner credibility matters; use the baker’s story without sounding like a generic big company.
- **Minimal forms, high-trust CTAs:** one primary custom-order CTA, one secondary contact/gallery CTA; avoid clutter.
- **Mobile-first spacing:** no negative-margin overlaps, no modals blocking the first impression, 44px+ tap targets.

## What Sirens Sweets should NOT copy

- Blocking popups/cookie modals on first load.
- Heavy ecommerce menus that bury custom-cake/contact actions.
- Generic stock-looking section grids.
- Overly loud colors that compete with the cake photos.

## Proposed Sirens Sweets direction

The strongest route is a hybrid: **Levain’s warm playful polish + Dominique Ansel’s editorial craft + Porto’s decision-first CTA structure**, with Sirens Sweets’ existing pastel/aqua/pink brand and real cake imagery.
